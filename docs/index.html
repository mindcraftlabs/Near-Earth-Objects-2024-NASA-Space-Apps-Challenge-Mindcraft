ABOUT THE CHALLENGE

Since a mechanical model of the solar system was presented to Charles Boyle, 4th Earl of Orrery, in 1713, such models have been referred to as orreries. The first orreries were physical models, but today we can use numerous tools to create virtual orreries that have many more features than their ancient mechanical counterparts. Your challenge is to create an interactive orrery web app that is embedded in a webpage and displays celestial bodies such as planets, Near-Earth Asteroids, Near-Earth Comets, and Potentially Hazardous Asteroids.

Problematic & Solution

Problematic:

Tracking celestial bodies, especially near-Earth objects like asteroids and comets, is essential for both scientific research and planetary defense. Traditional tools are often limited in their interactivity and accessibility for the general public.

Solution:

Develop a web-based orrery that provides real-time, interactive 3D visualizations of planets, Near-Earth Asteroids, comets, and other celestial objects. This platform will allow users to explore and understand the dynamic behavior of these bodies in an accessible and engaging way.
